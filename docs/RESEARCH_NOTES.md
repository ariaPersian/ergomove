# Research Notes

These notes are for product grounding, not for medical claims.

## Reliable starting sources

- CDC/NIOSH notes that office work should avoid over-reaching, prolonged sitting/standing, and awkward postures; it also notes that short breaks every hour can reduce discomfort for computer workers.
- OSHA computer workstation guidance lists neutral positioning: hands/wrists/forearms in line, head level, relaxed shoulders, elbows close to body, and supported feet.
- WHO physical activity guidance for adults recommends regular activity and reducing sedentary time.
- Mayo Clinic office ergonomics guidance covers chair height, equipment spacing, desk posture, monitor/keyboard placement, and workstation setup.

## Product implications

- Use reminders as nudges, not as medical prescriptions.
- Include job profiles because risk posture differs across office work, control-room work, call-center work, driving, and warehouse terminals.
- Keep break intervals configurable.
- Avoid aggressive blocking by default; start with gentle reminders and allow escalation.
- Add safety notes for each movement.

## Candidate reminder intervals

- Eye reminder: every 20 minutes, 20 seconds.
- Micro-posture reminder: every 30–45 minutes, 20–30 seconds.
- Movement reminder: every 45–60 minutes, 60–180 seconds.
- Longer rest break: every 2–3 hours, configurable.

## Content approval workflow

1. Draft reminder in English.
2. Translate to Persian.
3. Add category, job profiles, duration, safety note.
4. Review by ergonomics/physio/occupational-health expert before commercial release.
5. Version content packs.
